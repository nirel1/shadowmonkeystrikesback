Why We Care About FFTs

“FFTs require linear space-complexity for the prover, and prevent the prover from being implemented in logarithmic space as in the sumcheck protocol or other succinct arguments based on folding protocols. In contrast, we reduce $\mathsf{NP}$ statements to bilinear relations such as scalar-product relations, and then rely on succinct arguments for scalar products; this reduction can be performed via a linear number of cryptographic operations, and without relying on FFTs.”
